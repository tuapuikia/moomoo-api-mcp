# Sentiment Analysis Criteria

Use this reference to evaluate news events and their potential impact on stock price movements.

## 1. High-Impact Catalysts

### Positive (+)
- **Earnings Beat & Raise:** Reported earnings per share (EPS) and revenue exceed consensus, and the company raises its forward-looking guidance.
- **Product Launch/Partnership:** Major new product announcement or strategic partnership with a key industry leader.
- **Analyst Upgrade:** Significant analyst upgrade from 'Hold' to 'Buy' or a substantial increase in price targets from major firms (e.g., Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley).
- **Macro Tailwinds:** Fed interest rate cuts, positive sector-specific policy shifts, or easing inflation data.
- **M&A Activity:** Positive acquisition rumors or finalized deals that add clear value.

### Negative (-)
- **Earnings Miss & Lower Guidance:** Reported EPS/revenue fall short of consensus, or the company lowers its guidance.
- **Lawsuits/Regulatory Action:** SEC investigations, major class-action lawsuits, or unfavorable regulatory changes (e.g., antitrust).
- **Product Delay/Failure:** Recall of major products or significant delays in key releases.
- **Analyst Downgrade:** Major downgrade or price target cut from key analysts.
- **Macro Headwinds:** Rate hikes, geopolitical instability, or negative economic data (e.g., rising unemployment).

## 2. Evaluation Matrix

| Signal Intensity | Description | Action Recommendation |
|------------------|-------------|-----------------------|
| **Strong Bullish** | Multiple positive catalysts, high volume. | **PROPOSE BUY** |
| **Weak Bullish**   | Minor positive news, low volume.          | **MONITOR**     |
| **Neutral**        | No significant news or balanced signals. | **HOLD**        |
| **Weak Bearish**   | Minor negative news, moderate volume.     | **MONITOR**     |
| **Strong Bearish** | Major negative catalyst, high volume.    | **PROPOSE SELL**|

## 3. Source Credibility
- **Tier 1:** Official SEC filings (10-K, 10-Q, 8-K), company press releases.
- **Tier 2:** Major financial outlets (Bloomberg, Reuters, WSJ, CNBC).
- **Tier 3:** High-signal social (Reddit r/WallStreetBets for retail momentum, X for breaking news).
- **Tier 4:** Generic blogs or low-reputation news aggregators (use with caution).
